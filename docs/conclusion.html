Conclusion

The Monte Carlo method has proven to be a powerful and flexible tool for solving the linear Boltzmann equation. Its main strengths are:

  • Its ability to handle high-dimensional problems
  • Its simplicity of implementation
  • Its adaptability to complex geometries
  • The possibility to leverage parallelism to accelerate computations

In our case studies, we were able to observe its effectiveness in simulating various phenomena:

  • Particle advection in a flow
  • Absorption and radiative transport
  • Diffusive behavior in frequent collision regime

The obtained results showed very good agreement with analytical solutions when available.

Future Work

Several paths for improvement and extension of this project are possible:

  • Parallelization: Exploit parallel architectures (multi-core, GPU) to reduce computation times and handle larger problems.
  • Coupling: Couple the Boltzmann equation solution with other physics (e.g. thermal, fluid mechanics).
  • Experimental Validation: Compare simulation results with experimental data on well-defined test cases.

These developments will expand the method's field of application and allow tackling more complex problems where deterministic approaches show their limitations.
