Equation Context

Introduction

The Boltzmann equation is fundamental in the kinetic theory of gases, describing the statistical behavior of a thermodynamic system out of equilibrium. It governs the temporal evolution of the particle distribution function.

Mathematical Formulation

The equation describes the evolution of particle density $u(\mathbf{x}, t, \mathbf{v})$ under various phenomena:

$$ \frac{\partial u}{\partial t} + \mathbf{v} \cdot \nabla_{\mathbf{x}} u = -v \sigma_t(\mathbf{x}, \mathbf{v}) u(\mathbf{x}, t, \mathbf{v}) + \int v \sigma_s(\mathbf{x}, \mathbf{v}, \mathbf{v}') u(\mathbf{x}, t, \mathbf{v}') \, d\mathbf{v}' $$

Equation Terms

  • $u(\mathbf{x}, t, \mathbf{v})$: Particle density at position $\mathbf{x}$, time $t$, with velocity $\mathbf{v}$
  • $\sigma_t(\mathbf{x}, \mathbf{v})$: Total cross-section quantifying the total interaction rate
  • $\sigma_s(\mathbf{x}, \mathbf{v}, \mathbf{v}')$: Differential scattering cross-section
  • $\mathbf{v} \cdot \nabla_{\mathbf{x}} u$: Transport term representing free particle streaming
  • $-v \sigma_t u$: Absorption term
  • The integral term represents particle gain through scattering

Domain Configuration

The study domain $\Omega$ is typically a three-dimensional volume where particles evolve. Boundary and initial conditions are essential for solving the equation:

  • Initial condition: $u(\mathbf{x}, 0, \mathbf{v}) = u_0(\mathbf{x}, \mathbf{v})$
  • Boundary conditions: Depend on physical configuration (reflective, periodic, or inflow)

Fundamental Relations

An important relationship connects the different cross-sections:

$$ \sigma_t(\mathbf{x}, \mathbf{v}) = \sigma_a(\mathbf{x}, \mathbf{v}) + \int \sigma_s(\mathbf{x}, \mathbf{v}, \mathbf{v}') \, d\mathbf{v}' $$

where $\sigma_a$ represents the absorption cross-section.
